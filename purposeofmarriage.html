Understanding the

Purpose of Marriage

We see a lot of young people excited about getting married and they spend time fantasizing about their ideal life partner and even what their wedding day would look like.

This truly can be really exciting. That big wedding day comes with so much thrill.

I can still remember how much I enjoyed my wedding ceremony; we had it colourful and planned with fun activities like chair dance, hide and seek and lots more. Most thrilling was that I was actually getting married to my one and only lover, being his wife and finally enjoying sex wholesomely. On the other hand, beyond the wedding, there is marriage and then, before we know it, as some of us; we are pregnant!!

We become parents, having “mini us” influencing how we now live our lives; what we do and what we can not do, what we buy and what we can not buy, where we go and where we can not go, and when we sleep and when we can not sleep, whether we can return to a paid 9-5 job or not, the list is endless. Some days can be really good while others can be so overwhelming.

From Genesis 2: 18 The Lord God said, “It is not good for the man to be alone. I will make a helper suitable for him.”

One purpose of marriage is companionship which as a matter of fact is a beautiful thing. A number of people put in a lot of work to ensure that from the point of choosing a life partner, they choose who they would enjoy in quietness, conversations, intimacy, and in every other way. Even after the wedding, deliberate efforts are made to keep the marriage enjoyable.
Another purpose of marriage is bearing children, Genesis 1: 28 “Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and subdue it.

In all of these, It is imperative to understand the purpose of marriage because understanding its purpose is the compass that sets couples in the right direction

Unfortunately, not a lot of people choose life partners with this in mind (I mean having a consideration like; "is this the kind of person I want to raise children with?" sounds weird right?
This is a topic for another day, keep an eye on my blogposts ).
It would interest you to know that God is expecting more from us in this regard. From my studies, very few people prepare for parenting as singles and even when married. Most of the energy is channelled towards preparing for marriage; being the right person, finding the right person and how to make the marriage heaven on earth. This leaves a costly loophole in this second purpose of marriage. God doesn't just want us to bear children, he has a spec.

Malachi 2 : 15
"But did He not make them one, Having a remnant of the Spirit? And why one? He seeks godly offspring. Therefore take heed to your spirit, And let none deal treacherously with the wife of his youth."

At a certain time, Prophet Malachi delivered a message of judgment to the people of Judah who had turned away from the true worship of the Lord, leaving themselves under judgment and in need of salvation. God told the people exactly where they had fallen short and what He expected of them. From Malachi 2 : 15, we see that the reason God ordains marriage as an irrevocable union is that He seeks godly children. Our responsibility as christian parents is to deliberately train our children to be godly despite the permissiveness of sin and unruliness in the world we live in today. Knowing this calls for setting and prioritizing our parenting goals correctly.

The question now is; how does God expect us to do this?

Let's see Deuteronomy 6: 6 - 9 Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God is one Lord: 6 And these words, which I command thee this day, shall be in thine heart: 7 And thou shalt teach them diligently unto thy children, and shalt talk of them when thou sittest in thine house, and when thou walkest by the way, and when thou liest down, and when thou risest up. 8 And thou shalt bind them for a sign upon thine hand, and they shall be as frontlets between thine eyes. 9 And thou shalt write them upon the posts of thy house, and on thy gates.

God’s word must be everywhere and all around our homes and children’s lives starting from when we conceive them. Studies have proven that babies in the womb are able to recognise voices, so we should read God’s word to them aloud.

Let them hear God’s word audibly and learn to recognise it early so they can follow. John 10:27 My sheep hear my voice,and I know them,and they follow me

Parenting is not a walk in the park, however, as parents, we must make concerted efforts to be intentional about the values & content we inculcate in our children.

My testimony is that of a parent who is intentional about displaying content & inscriptions about God all around my children so that they eat, breathe and sleep in God’s magnificent word. I do this by pasting scriptures on their walls and buying toys, t-shirts and games with God’s words inscribed on them.

From my womb, the scriptures were read aloud and continue to be read aloud to them. I placed scripture cards in their cots while they slept and now that they are of school age, I give them the cards to take to school.

This is similar to the principle Jacob used on animals (that have no spirit) yet they were able to conceive what they saw, how much more are our children who have a spirit and a soul beholding God’s word that is quick and powerful. The result of that contact has been life-transforming for my children.

With all glory to God, I can say my children are exceptional and God-conscious.

My husband bought them complete bibles that they are happy to take to school and to church. As a mother, I continue to encourage my children that loving the Lord and walking in His ways is the coolest thing ever, particularly because of the world we currently live in.

Their desires, conversations and playtime are always biblically inclined and it amazes me. My 7year old daughter was asked to create a story on slides for her ICT project and when I read her story it was that of Abraham and Sarah; though she gave the characters different names. I see this juxtaposition play out a lot. One day, second daughter at 4years old was walking into the bathroom one night, it was dark and she was obviously scared. I could hear her say repeatedly from where I was that "I do not have the spirit of fear but of power, love and a sound mind" while she carefully went into the bathroom. Even my young son believes in rebuking anything that comes in his way, even flies. His childlike faith in God is incredible.
